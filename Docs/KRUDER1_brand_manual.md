# KRUDER1  
## Brand Identity Manual

---

## 1. Brand Positioning

Kruder1 exists for photo booth owners who already understand one thing:  
the problem is not the booth — the problem is competing with the same offer as everyone else.

Kruder1 does not help you lower prices.  
It gives you something worth charging more for.

This is not a tool to survive the market.  
It is a tool to exit the price war.

---

## 2. Target Audience

### Core Client

- Photo booth owner (business owner, not employee)
- Already selling events, but:
  - Tired of competing on price
  - Frustrated by generic offers in the industry
  - Feels the market is stuck with the same ideas
- Commercial mindset (seller, not technician)
- Not afraid to invest if ROI is clear
- Wants to be early, not late
- Wants to charge better, not work more

**They want a legitimate reason to level up.**

---

### Non-Ideal Client (Self-Filtered)

- People looking for the cheapest option
- Anyone trying to copy what everyone else does
- Those who need excessive hand-holding
- Owners who refuse to rethink pricing

Kruder1 does not reject them.  
They reject themselves.

---

## 3. The Enemy (Ideological)

Kruder1 is against:

- Price wars
- Generic photo booth experiences
- Overcomplicated, poorly designed software
- An industry stuck in “the same as always”
- The mindset of “this is how it’s always been done”

Internal statement:

> Kruder1 exists because we’re tired of seeing talented photo booth owners  
> competing as if their work had no value.

---

## 4. Brand Promise

Kruder1 promises:

- Real differentiation (not marketing noise)
- Radical simplicity (fast to understand, easy to use)
- A tool that changes the client conversation
- Higher perceived value
- A product that is charged for, not justified

Kruder1 does **not** promise:

- Automatic clients
- Guaranteed bookings
- Overnight success
- Magical results

---

## 5. Relationship With Money

Core belief:

> It’s not expensive.  
> It’s profitable if you know how to sell.

Kruder1 is not measured by its price,  
but by what it allows you to charge.

If someone can’t see the ROI,  
it’s not a pricing problem — it’s a vision problem.

---

## 6. Brand Personality

### Kruder1 Is

- Confident
- Calm
- Direct
- Elegant
- Functional
- Built from real experience

### Kruder1 Is Not

- A generic startup
- “AI buzzword” marketing
- Aggressive sales-driven
- Empty hype
- Overly technical
- Over-explanatory

Kruder1 speaks little.  
But when it speaks, people listen.

---

## 7. Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Voice

- Confident
- Calmly dominant
- Never defensive
- Never apologetic
- Never needy

### Tone

- Direct
- Filters clients naturally
- Subtly challenges ego
- Premium without saying “premium”

Golden rule:

> If it sounds like it’s trying to convince, it’s wrong.

---

## 8. Core Brand Statements (Anchor Phrases)

These ideas define all future copy:

- **You don’t sell photos.  
  You sell differentiation.**

- **The photo booth isn’t the problem.  
  Offering the same thing as everyone else is.**

- **Kruder1 doesn’t get you clients.  
  It gives you something worth paying more for.**

- **It’s not for everyone.  
  And that’s part of the value.**

---

## 9. Exclusivity Communication

Never communicate hard limits or numbers.

Always communicate exclusivity as intent:

- “Not mass-market”
- “Not for everyone”
- “Designed for those who understand differentiation”
- “We’re not chasing volume”

This creates:
- Scarcity
- Desire
- FOMO
- Long-term flexibility (software today, hardware tomorrow)

---

## 10. Language Strategy

- English is the primary language (structure, headlines)
- Spanish is equally important, not a poor translation
- Both languages must sound:
  - Natural
  - Confident
  - Premium

Avoid:
- Cheap Spanglish
- Literal translations without intent

---

## 11. Long-Term Vision (3 Years)

Kruder1 will be known as the Mexican brand that raised the photo booth standard in LATAM,  
with beautifully designed, highly functional products  
made for owners who understand return on investment.

---

## 12. Final Brand Test

If the brand is working, a photo booth owner should think:

- “This is different”
- “This isn’t for everyone”
- “If I use this right, it pays for itself”
- “I want to be among the first”
- “This is exactly what I needed to get out of that zone”

If these thoughts don’t happen, the message is wrong.
